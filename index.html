🧭 1. The Philosophy of Home Education
🎯 Your Goal
You are not trying to replicate school at home. You are building a thinker, a reader, and a capable human being. School teaches to the middle. You teach to this specific child. That is your superpower.

Aim for: depth over coverage, mastery over speed, curiosity over compliance.
📐 The 80/20 Rule
80% of academic success comes from:
  • Reading fluency — a child who reads well learns everything faster
  • Mathematical fluency — number sense unlocks all STEM
  • Writing clearly — organises thought and communicates ideas
Everything else builds on these three. Do them daily. Never skip them.
🏠 Life IS the Classroom
Cooking = maths + chemistry + life skills.
Shopping = budgeting + maths + social skills.
A walk = geography + biology + PE.

Homeschool has a secret weapon: context. Use it every day.
🚀 Mastery-Based Acceleration
A child who masters Grade 3 Maths is ready for Grade 4 Maths — regardless of age. This system tracks mastery per subject, so your child can be at Grade 6 Maths and Grade 3 Geography simultaneously. Never let age limit learning.
⏰ 2. The Ideal Daily Routine
Golden Rule: Consistency beats perfection. The same start time, same order of subjects, same end-time routine builds automatic learning habits within 3 weeks.

🧪 3. The 4-Step Lesson Method
1
Teach & Explain (15 min) — Introduce the concept using a real-life example first. Never start abstract. Before fractions, cut an apple. Before place value, use coins.
2
Guided Practice (20 min) — Work through 3–5 examples together. Think aloud. Let the child complete the final step each time.
3
Independent Check (10 min) — Give exactly 5 questions. Child works alone. 5/5 = move on. 3-4/5 = review tomorrow. Under 3/5 = reteach now.
4
Real-Life Connection (5 min) — Ask: "Where will you see this in real life?" Then show it. Maths → count change. Writing → label objects.
✅ 4. What Works vs What Doesn't
✅ DO These Things
  • Start with maths every single morning
  • Read aloud together — even to older children
  • Ask "why?" and "how do you know?" constantly
  • Celebrate effort, not just results
  • Use mastery checkpoints before moving on
  • Connect every lesson to real life
  • Allow the child to teach YOU what they learned
  • Let the child's curiosity lead bonus learning
  • Give brain breaks — physical movement matters
❌ NEVER Do These
  • Rush through content to "finish the curriculum"
  • Move on before a topic is mastered
  • Allow screens during learning time
  • Teach for more than 90 mins without a break
  • Compare this child to any other child
  • Lose your temper during a lesson — stop, reset
  • Skip maths or English "because we'll catch up"
  • Label the child as "bad at" any subject permanently
